INDOOR SPORTS

  1. LUDO

Precepts:

  1. The entrants would have to participate individually in the event.
  2. Each entrant must take one of the four colour and a single die must be thrown to determine the movements.
  3. Place your 4 pieces in the corner of the same colour. 
  4. Determine the first player by rolling the die. Whoever, rolls the highest number takes the first turn of the game
  5. Roll a 6 to move a piece out of your base onto the main track. Roll the die a second time after each time you get a 6. End your turn after moving a piece if you didn’t roll a 6.
  6.  Land in a space with an opponent's piece to send it back to their base.
  7.  Move onto the same space as one of your pieces to make a block. 
  8. Try to land on marked safe spaces (if your board has them).
  9. Move your pieces into your home column once you go around the board.
  10. Roll the exact number you need to get pieces into the finishing square.
  11. Win the game by moving all 4 of your pieces to the finishing square.
  12. Be accountable: Take responsibility for your actions and outcomes during the challenge. Do not blame others for your mistakes or shortcomings.

4. CHESS

 PRECEPTS:

  1. Chess is played on a board with 64 squares arranged in an 8x8 grid.

  1. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.

  1. The objective is to put the opponent's king in "checkmate" - a situation where the king is in a position to be captured (i.e., in "check") and there is no legal move to get out of check.

  1. The pieces move in specific ways: the king can move one square in any direction, the queen can move any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically, the rook can move any number of squares horizontally or vertically, the bishop can move any number of squares diagonally, the knight moves in an L-shape, and pawns move forward one square or capture diagonally one square ahead on either side.

  1. The game can end in a draw if both players agree, if there is a stalemate, if there is insufficient material to checkmate (e.g., only kings left on the board), or if a player claims a draw due to the threefold repetition rule or the 50-move rule.

  1. Players take turns making moves, with white moving first. Players cannot make moves that put their own king in check.

  1.  The game ends when one player's king is in checkmate, or when a draw is agreed upon or declared.

  1. BHAAG MILKHA 100m

PRECEPTS:

1. Every 100 m sprinter must begin the race with his feet in the starting blocks.

2.The official race starter will call the sprinters to their blocks and on command the runners will adopt a set position.

3. The set position requires to runner have both feet in the block and adopt a position with the body weight on their hands.

4.On the starter's call begin the race.

5.A false start is called when the feet of the runner leave the starting blocks before the starter call.

6. Any 100 m sprinter who leaves her lane or obstructs the path oof another sprinter will be automatically disqualified from the race.

7. In these circumstances, the race referee can order the race to be run over again if it is deemed necessary.

8. The finish is the decisive stage of the race,  and is very exciting when sprinters are close to each other in terms of ability.
